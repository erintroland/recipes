Raspberry Buns

February 17, 2020

Best to do over two days. You need about 2.5-3 hours at the house so someone can mind the buns. There is an hour of the first rise though, to do something else, but then you have to roll the dough out, fill it, and slice it.

There are also some cool sweet and salty variations at the end of the recipe that would be fun to try.

For next time: They came out a little dry. 1. Make sure the raspberry jam is cold. A lot of it ran out the sides when I cut them. Also try to be as gentle as possible when slicing so as not to lose jam. 2. Don't forget the egg wash! I suspect this helps the buns stay moist.
