Pressure Cooker Pot Roast

January 28, 2018

2-3lb chuck roast (the one I cooked was 2.5) Flour Oil 1.5-2 c liquid (ex: ck broth, red wine, and water combo) 5 carrots 2 celery stalks 4 potatoes 2 onions Pepper Salt Herbes de Provence 3 whole cloves

1. Trim fat from beef. Really do this well because otherwise there are lots of gross gelatinous pocket of fat in the finished product. You'll need to cut into two pieces to fit into the Instant Pot.

2. Coat meat on all sides with flour, salt and pepper. It's easy if you put out some plastic wrap, put the meat on it, and use thongs to both flip the meat around and rub in the seasonings so you avoid touching the raw meat with your hands during this part.

3. Heat oil in Instant Pot. Add beef and brown on all sides.

4. Chop coarsely carrots, celery. Trim outer part of onion. Leave whole both the onions and potatoes. Maybe add a little salt at this point.

5. Once beef is brown, add liquid. Scrape bottom of pot. Add everything else. Stir.

6. Cook on high pressure for 60 minutes. Let is release naturall for 10-15 mins before doing quick release.

Of course, there are many variations in what veggies to use as well as what liquid and herbs etc.

Notes: 1. 5 cloves were overpowering, so cut to 3. 2. I added in fresh sage--I couldn't taste it. Probably better to add dried thyme or oregano.
