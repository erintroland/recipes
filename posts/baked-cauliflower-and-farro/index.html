Baked Cauliflower and Farro

March 19, 2017

Wow, this was incredibly delicious! It's also very easy to make, though it took me about an hour start to finish (prep only, then 20 ins of baking). It's easy to prep as you go, chopping the cauliflower while the (quick cooking) farro is cooking, grating the cheese while the cauliflower is sauteing, etc. Saute everything in the cast iron, bake it in the 9 x 13 pan.

1. I added some chopped spinach and white beans. 2. Cut back on the salt--don't add any when you're sauteing the cauliflower, then try 1/2 teaspoon when you mix everything together (half of what the recipe calls for). Also make sure to drain the capers. 3. There is such a thing as too much sage. The second time I made this I really put in too much. Measure those two tablespoons! 4. It's almost better reheated--it was a little dry straight out of the oven. Could consider adding in some cream (usually on hand!) or maybe just more ricotta swirled into the casserole mixture itself rather than dolloped in the middle. 5. You can use a full bag of TJ's quick cooking farro and a full block of Aldi Havarti cheese. With the spinach and white beans, it all fits in the 9 x 13 dish. 6. This will make the kitchen floor messy! Cauliflower etc everywhere. Just a heads up:)
