Liege Waffles

March 24, 2024

A pain to make but they did turn out delicious. Eat them right off the iron as soon as they're not scalding. You need to start these the night before because they need a lot of time to rise. Too much work to do all in the morning.

  1. Make sure to let the butter soften a bit before you start.
  2. Made a half recipe for 6 total waffles.
  3. Heat up the milk a bit so it's a better environment for the yeast.
  4. You don't need a stand mixer--pretty easy to do by hand. Whisk the wet ingredients. Use a spatula when incorporating the dry ingredients, then use your hands. Especially incorporating the butter, it softens in your hands and works its way in pretty easily.
  5. You also don't need these to come up to room temp after they come out of the fridge in the morning. 30 mins was enough time.
  6. Use less pearl sugar--maybe cut by a fourth or a third--these were pretty darn sweet.
  7. To cook: Heat the waffle iron up to a bit below medium, then check after 4 mins.
  8. It is a bit of a pain to clean the waffle iron because of the burnt sugar. Try and get the big globs off while it's hot, then use a warm soapy paper towel and your fingers to get the rest of it out.
