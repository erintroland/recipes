Bread Stuffing Acorn Squash

November 10, 2019

Wow this was so delicious, simple, and versatile! You mix the stuffing, cut the squash in half, brush with olive oil S&P, stuff with the stuffing, then bake. Cooking the squash cut side down makes the stuffing as brown and crusty as possible:)

Methods: To cut open the squash, use chef knife to one side of the stem, slice down until you can eventually just pull the squash apart into two pieces (you can't cut through the stem it's too tough). You can eat the skin as it does become soft, but sometimes that's a bit uneven and there may be a few skin chunks that are too tough to eat.

Ingredients: I skipped the bacon, added some white beans by mashing in to the rest of the stuffing when I mixed it by hand, plus added some chicken stock to the shallots when I sauteed them. I substituted thyme for sage since I had it one hand, flavor kind of disappeared. I also had half and half on hand which I subbed for the heavy cream.

You could add different aromatics (onions, leeks), different beans (garbanzo, kidney), different herbs (rosemary, sage), different cheese (cheddar, gruyere, fontina, etc) and it would turn out great again!
